About Me

I grew up in Nepal where my curiosity about technology first began. From an early age I was fascinated by computers and the possibilities they create for communication, innovation, and problem solving. That curiosity gradually developed into a strong interest in understanding how systems work and how software can be built to solve meaningful problems.

Moving to the United States as an international student was both an exciting and challenging experience. It pushed me to adapt to new environments, cultures, and academic expectations. These experiences helped me develop independence, resilience, and a strong motivation to pursue opportunities that contribute to both personal and professional growth.

At Weber State University I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with minors in both Data Analytics and Cybersecurity & Network Management. Through my coursework and projects I have gained experience in programming, database systems, networking, and analytical thinking. I enjoy learning how different technologies connect together to build efficient and scalable systems.

Beyond academics, I believe mentorship and community engagement are important parts of growth. I enjoy working with students and organizations to create opportunities for learning and collaboration within technology and STEM fields.

My Journey

My journey into technology developed gradually through curiosity, education, and real-world experience. Each role I have taken on has helped me understand different aspects of how technology interacts with people, communities, and organizations.

One of my early professional roles was working as a Web Specialist. In this role I focused on developing website layouts and improving digital user experiences. It helped me understand how thoughtful design and clean implementation can improve how users interact with digital systems.

Later, as a Community Partner Specialist, I collaborated with organizations and university partners to coordinate outreach initiatives that connected people with resources and opportunities. This role strengthened my ability to communicate, organize events, and work with diverse groups toward shared goals.

Currently I serve as an EAST Mentor where I support engineering and technology students by providing guidance, mentorship, and academic support. This experience allows me to share what I have learned while continuing to grow as a leader and collaborator within the STEM community.
